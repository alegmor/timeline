Study C · Assessing the continuum solver

Bubble departure benchmark

Study C is where we understand the continuum side of the coupling: how a volume-of-fluid (VOF) solver defines the liquid–vapour interface, how surface tension is modelled on that diffuse interface, where its spurious currents come from, and what the solver is missing for the coupling role. The vehicle is interThermalPhaseChangeFoam run on a bubble-departure benchmark — the saturated ethanol pool-boiling experiment of Kaniowski (2018).

The case, reduced to one cavity
  • The microchannel surface (MC-0.3-0.2-0.6) is reduced to a single nucleation site — one axisymmetric cavity, revolved about the axis into a thin 5° wedge, preserving the 3D volume scaling of an isolated bubble.
  • The cavity is initialised as vapour, the pool as saturated liquid — a pre-existing liquid–vapour interface for the phase-change model to act on.
  • Wall superheat is imposed directly as a fixed temperature (ΔT = 5 K); conjugate heat transfer in the solid is not resolved.
  • Phase change: interface-equilibrium closure; surface tension: smoothed continuum-surface-force (Brackbill 1992); interface advection: vanLeer + MULES.
