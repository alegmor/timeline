Multipseudopotential approach

1. Evolution equation

One lattice Boltzmann update, split into four physical roles.

\[ \underbrace{f_i(\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{c}_i\Delta t,\,t+\Delta t) - f_i(\mathbf{x},t)}_{\text{streaming}} \;=\; \underbrace{\omega\,\Omega^{(\mathrm{BGK})}(f_i)}_{\text{collision}} \;+\; \underbrace{S_i(\mathbf{x},t)}_{\text{forcing}} \;+\; \underbrace{\textcolor{#b30000}{\eta_i(\mathbf{x},t)}}_{\textcolor{#b30000}{\text{thermal noise}}} \]
Streaming

Populations hop along the lattice links to neighbouring sites.

Collision

BGK relaxation of \(f_i\) toward local equilibrium.

Forcing

Injects the Shan–Chen attraction \(S_i\) that drives phase separation.

Thermal noise

Stochastic term \(\eta_i\) — present only in the fluctuating extension (Study B).

2. The Shan–Chen force

Each site attracts its neighbours; the strength is set by the “stickiness” \(\psi(n)\).

\[ \mathbf{F}^{SC}(\mathbf{x}) = -G\,\psi(\mathbf{x}) \sum_i w_i\,\psi(\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{c}_i\Delta t)\,\mathbf{c}_i \]

The coupling constant \(G\) sets the interaction strength (with \(G<0\) giving cohesion), and \(\psi(\mathbf{x})\) is the pseudopotential evaluated from the local density. Summing over the lattice directions \(\mathbf{c}_i\) with weights \(w_i\) produces a net attraction toward denser neighbours — the microscopic origin of surface tension.

3. How to introduce a real equation of state

The lattice pressure that \(\psi\) generates, term by term.

\[ P_N(x) = \underbrace{n c_s^2}_{\text{ideal gas}} + \underbrace{\tfrac{G c_s^2 e_2}{2}\psi^2}_{\text{non-ideal bulk (the EOS)}} + \underbrace{G c_s^4\Big[\bar\alpha\big(\tfrac{d\psi}{dx}\big)^2 + \bar\beta\,\psi\tfrac{d^2\psi}{dx^2}\Big]}_{\text{interface: surface tension}} \]
Ideal gas

\(n c_s^2\) — the baseline lattice pressure.

Non-ideal bulk

\(\tfrac{G c_s^2 e_2}{2}\psi^2\) — the term that makes the fluid non-ideal: the EOS.

Interface

Gradient terms setting surface tension; \(\bar\alpha,\bar\beta\) are Lulli’s tunable knobs.

Multipseudopotential (MPI). A single consistent \(\psi\) is too rigid to reproduce a real fluid. Superposing several pseudopotentials gives

\[ P_b = n c_s^2 + \tfrac{c_s^2 e_2}{2}\sum_i G_i\,\psi_i^2(n), \qquad \psi_i(n)=\Big(\tfrac{n}{\varepsilon_i+n}\Big)^{1/\varepsilon_i}, \]

choosing the set \(\{G_i,\varepsilon_i\}\) so that the sum matches a chosen (arbitrary) equation of state.
