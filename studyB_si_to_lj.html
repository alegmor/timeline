From SI to the LJ reduced-unit

The Lennard-Jones potential — where the scales come from

All three reduction scales are read straight off the pair potential that defines the reference fluid. Two atoms a distance \(r\) apart interact through the Lennard-Jones 12–6 potential shown here.

  • \(\sigma\) — length scale. The separation at which \(U=0\); effectively the molecular diameter. The steep \(r^{-12}\) term is short-range repulsion (atoms can’t overlap).
  • \(\varepsilon\) — energy scale. The depth of the attractive well, reached at \(r_{\min}=2^{1/6}\sigma\). The \(r^{-6}\) term is the long-range van der Waals attraction.
  • \(m\) — mass scale. The mass of one particle; it sets the natural time \(\tau_{\mathrm{LJ}}=\sigma\sqrt{m/\varepsilon}\).

Because every LJ quantity is built from \(\sigma,\varepsilon,m\), dividing by the right combination of them makes each variable dimensionless — the reduced units below.

Reduced units of the LJ fluid

The previous step ties the lattice model to the Lennard-Jones reference fluid’s own units. The LJ fluid is dimensionless in three molecular scales:

\[ \sigma\ (\text{length}),\quad \varepsilon\ (\text{energy}),\quad m\ (\text{mass}) \]

so every quantity has a reduced form:

\[ T^{*}=\frac{k_BT}{\varepsilon},\quad \rho^{*}=\rho\,\sigma^{3},\quad p^{*}=\frac{p\,\sigma^{3}}{\varepsilon} \]
\[ t^{*}=\frac{t}{\tau_{\mathrm{LJ}}},\qquad \tau_{\mathrm{LJ}}=\sigma\sqrt{m/\varepsilon} \]

The MBWR closure and the MD reference rate are both stated in these reduced units. All the simulation scales in Study B and Study D — domain size, density, temperature, and time — are defined this way, in units of \(\sigma\), \(\varepsilon\) and \(m\).

Recovering SI units

The LJ–argon convention
Give \(\sigma,\varepsilon,m\) the values of argon — the three numbers that fix every SI scale. Argon is used because its monatomic, non-polar atom is almost a perfect single-site LJ fluid, which is why it is the standard reference for nucleation studies.
\[ \sigma_{\mathrm{Ar}} \approx 0.34\ \mathrm{nm},\qquad \varepsilon_{\mathrm{Ar}}/k_B \approx 120\ \mathrm{K} \]
So a domain quoted as \(100\,\sigma\) is about \(100\times0.34\ \mathrm{nm}\approx \mathbf{34\ nm}\) of physical space.

The extracted reduced rate then becomes SI:

\[ J \;=\; \frac{J^{*}}{\sigma^{3}\,\tau_{\mathrm{LJ}}}\quad[\mathrm{m^{-3}\,s^{-1}}] \]
  • Benchmark target (Diemand 2014): \(J\approx 10^{27}\!-\!10^{31}\ \mathrm{m^{-3}\,s^{-1}}\) over \(T/T_c\approx 0.85\!-\!0.95\).
